Farmer Subsidy Schemes

  1. Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN)
    Provides ₹6,000 per year in three equal installments directly to farmers' bank accounts.
  2. Soil Health Card Scheme
    Helps farmers check soil quality and nutrient levels for better crop productivity.
  3. Crop Insurance Scheme (PMFBY)
    Protects farmers against crop losses due to natural calamities, pests, and diseases.
  4. Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanization
    Provides subsidies for farm machinery and tools to reduce labor costs.
  5. Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY)
    Supports states in strengthening agriculture and allied sectors through projects.
  6. Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY)
    Promotes organic farming with financial support for organic inputs and certification.
  7. National Food Security Mission (NFSM)
    Aims to increase production of rice, wheat, pulses, and coarse cereals sustainably.
  8. Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana
    Crop insurance with low premiums and faster claim settlements for farmers.
  9. Micro Irrigation Scheme
    Encourages farmers to adopt drip and sprinkler irrigation systems with subsidies.
  10. Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY)
    “Har Khet Ko Pani” – aims to provide water to every farm and improve irrigation efficiency.
  11. Interest Subsidy Scheme
    Provides interest subsidy on crop loans to reduce the financial burden on farmers.
  12. Kisan Credit Card Scheme (KCC)
    Offers easy credit to farmers for crop cultivation and other farming needs.
  13. National Horticulture Mission
    Supports farmers in horticulture crops like fruits, vegetables, flowers, and spices.
  14. Soil & Water Conservation Scheme
    Focuses on sustainable farming through watershed management and conservation.
  15. Organic Farming Promotion
    Encourages chemical-free farming by supporting bio-fertilizers and compost.

Motor Control 💧

The Motor Control feature allows farmers to remotely operate their irrigation pumps. This helps in saving time, labor, and water. You can turn the motor ON or OFF directly from this portal, ensuring crops get adequate water without manual intervention.

  • Turn ON: Start your irrigation system remotely.
  • Turn OFF: Stop the pump after watering to save electricity and water.
  • Smart Usage: Combine with soil moisture sensors or weather alerts to irrigate only when necessary.

Tip: Consistent irrigation improves crop yield, reduces water wastage, and lowers electricity costs.
